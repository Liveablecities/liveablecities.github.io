Livable Cities Research Lab

Our vision is to be an international leader in the area of engineering for livable cities and leverage off our unique position in the city of Auckland within Aotearoa New Zealand.

The University of Auckland’s Engineering for Livable Cities Research Lab is dedicated to engaging in research and contributing to workforce capacity in the area of engineering for liveable cities, including both the fundamental science as well as engineering solutions for smart, green and healthy cities.

Ultimately we aim to help address the gap in research needed to fulfil New Zealand’s Infrastructure Commission 30-year vision for infrastructure in which aspects of liveabilitylivablity and environmental aspects feature strongly by building on our international expertise and experience.

We aim to contribute to the aspirations and expectations of Treasury’s New Zealand Living Standards Framework (LSF), a framework to understand the drivers of wellbeing, as well as contributing to The United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) including New Zealand commitment to “fight inequalities and build peaceful, just and sustainable societies”.

Since prior to the COVID-19 pandemic, ‘engineering solutions for public healthhealth’ is considered to be a new hot topic requiring skills/knowledge gap in this area with respect to being able to design and deliver ‘liveable’ infrastructure, particularly in light of the critical role that infrastructure development plays in helping to rebuild the economy in the wake of the COVID-19 and fulfilling our commitment to the SDGs. It is a good time to be positioning ourselves internationally in the area of engineering for liveable cities at a time when the spotlight is on New Zealand because of our success in eliminating COVID-19.
